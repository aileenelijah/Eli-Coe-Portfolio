02  ·  Enterprise Voice System — What I’d Contribute

Three places enterprise
copy breaks. Fixed.

Break 01 — The claim outpaces the proof.

“Enterprise-grade governance” lands in the reader’s mental discard pile before the sentence ends. “Customer prompts aren’t used to train our models by default, and retention is configurable to your requirements” — that one they write down. The specific fact earns the trust. The label just borrows it.

Break 02 — The copy avoids what the reader is actually feeling.

Enterprise buyers aren’t looking for a partner to share their risk. They’re looking for infrastructure they can stand behind when the audit happens. Copy that softens the responsibility — makes deployment feel low-stakes, reversible, shared — misreads the room. The buyer who trusts you is the one you told the truth to.

Break 03 — The register shifts when the audience gets harder.

Onboarding copy sounds like a person. Error states sound like a lawyer. Campaign headlines sound like a brand. Product pages sound like a datasheet. Every surface break is a small trust erosion. The discipline is the same voice at every temperature — the subject changes, the register doesn’t.

What I look for when a voice is broken

  • Copy that talks about the product instead of to the person.
  • Claims made without the specific fact that earns them.
  • Touchpoints that sound like different brands — onboarding warm, error states robotic, ads performative.
  • Enterprise copy that inflates instead of earns: “transformative,” “enterprise-grade,” “at scale.”
  • Readiness assumptions — copy written for a customer who’s already convinced, not the one who hasn’t decided yet.

04  ·  Diagnostic Work — Messaging Problems, Fixed

The diagnosis comes
before the copy.

Copy that doesn’t work usually has a problem underneath the problem. These show the diagnosis before the fix — because if you don’t identify what’s actually wrong, the rewrite just covers it up.

01 — Feature-heavy product messaging

Copy describes what the product does without connecting to what the user actually wants. The product is positioned against competitors instead of against the user’s existing frustration.

Before
Our platform provides innovative solutions to streamline your workflow and enhance productivity outcomes.
After
You already know what’s slowing you down. We help you fix it — without adding three more tools to your stack.
The original speaks about the product. The rewrite speaks to the experience — and pre-empts the tool-fatigue objection in the same breath.

02 — Transactional lifecycle messaging

Confirmation copy moves information but doesn’t build the relationship. Every system message is a micro-interaction. Wasted micro-interactions erode trust slowly and invisibly.

Before
Your account has been successfully updated.
After
You’re all set. We’ll let you know if anything needs your attention.
The original confirms a system action. The rewrite confirms an emotional state — and removes the burden of follow-up from the user entirely. Clearer copy, yes. Also a different relationship.

03 — Re-engagement email leading with the task

The user has already demonstrated hesitation by not completing setup. Reminding them of the incomplete task reinforces the obligation without addressing the belief that created the hesitation.

Before
Complete your setup to start using [product].
After
Subject: You’re closer than you think. — You’re already set up. Most people start seeing results within minutes of logging back in. The only thing left is to take a look.
The original frames the situation as incomplete. The rewrite frames it as already resolved — and addresses the underlying belief before the user has a chance to act on it.

04 — Health and wellness category reframe

Copy leads with the solution before resolving the customer’s existing belief. The wall goes up before the sentence is finished.

Before
Boost your energy naturally with our scientifically formulated supplement.
After
You don’t have an energy problem. You have a processing problem.
Every other energy supplement is solving the same stated problem. This rewrite names a different problem — one the customer recognizes as more accurate. The category reframe is the entry point.

05 — Enterprise AI vendor using generic claims

The brand has a real differentiator and is burying it under the same language as companies that have none of those things.

Before
Our enterprise-grade AI platform delivers transformative, scalable results for organizations of all sizes, with security and compliance built in.
After
Every Anthropic employee — including legal, finance, and safety research — works on Claude Enterprise under the same governance model we offer customers. We don’t offer a different tier of trust.
The original is indistinguishable from every competitor. The rewrite earns the trust claim by proving it with a specific, verifiable fact.
05  ·  Applied Work — Real Brands, Real Stakes

The copy wasn’t
in the job description.
I did it anyway.

At every venue I managed, the copy fell to me — because I saw where it was weak and knew I could fix it — the copy fell to me, not by assignment. Cocktail menus that didn’t match the room. Social copy that sold a vibe the place didn’t have. Event language that should have made you want to be there and didn’t. I took it on because it’s my strength and because leaving it wrong wasn’t an option when I was the one responsible for the room.

Running operations and owning the brand voice simultaneously produces something a traditional copy background doesn’t: the knowledge of whether the language actually works. Every shift was a live test. Every weekend was a verdict.

Rosemary & Beauty Queen

Nashville bar and restaurant · On-site General Manager · rosemaryandbeautyqueen.com
The Role

On-site General Manager — operations, inventory, staff, and special events. The menu development and cocktail concepts weren’t a side project or a freelance engagement. They were part of running the place, because a GM who understands the brand is the only one who can write a menu that the room will actually sell.

The Copy Work

Original cocktail concepts and menu development for RBQ. The name carries dual personality — the softness of rosemary against the audacity of beauty queen — and the menu had to match that register without explaining it. REMAKE/REMODEL. THREE & NINE. AVALON. Each name is a brief that fits in three words: what it tastes like, what it feels like, and what it says about where you are. Written by someone who had also just finished the inventory count and had a reservation sheet to manage.

A menu written by a GM who runs the floor reads differently from one written by a copywriter who visited once. Every word has to be sellable by a server at 9pm on a Saturday.

Writing for both in the same week — and making neither sound like the other — is the practical definition of brand range. The operational background wasn’t separate from the copy work. 06  ·  Who I Am

Outside the box
is where I live.

Most people who apply for a role like this spent a decade inside the system that produces writers for roles like this. Agencies. In-house teams. Career ladders with defined rungs. Competence is the output. So is constraint — learned patterns, institutional caution, a muscle memory for what’s acceptable that is almost impossible to unlearn.

I don’t have any of that. Every brief I’ve ever taken, I’ve taken alone. Every standard I’ve held myself to, I set. There’s no tougher creative director than the one you answer to when you’re the only person in the room. No easier place to coast, and no faster feedback loop when the work isn’t good enough. What self-employment builds — if you take it seriously, and I have — is a work ethic and a creative standard that doesn’t require external pressure to activate. It’s just on, all the time.

The background looks unconventional on paper. Politics before I knew it was brand strategy. Novels before I understood they were voice training. Copy work across more categories than most specialists ever touch, without the specialization ever narrowing what I could see. My mind doesn’t stop working a problem. It turns it over, comes back to it, finds the angle that wasn’t visible from the front.

The certifications I didn’t have, I went and got. The gaps the market kept surfacing — SEO, content strategy, email marketing, analytics — I closed them, one by one, because the standard I hold myself to required it, not because someone asked. HubSpot in all three. GA4. WordPress. Identified, scheduled, finished.

I use Claude every day as a thinking partner — the way this role demands. I understand from the inside what it feels like when the model is working with you versus running past you. Writing enterprise copy for a company whose product is collaborative intelligence is a brief that rewards knowing that difference. I know it.
