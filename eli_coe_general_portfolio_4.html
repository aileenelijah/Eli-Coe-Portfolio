In a world teeming with AI voice and plagued with loneliness, connection is vital.

Insincere copy lands false even when the audience can’t say why. They feel it before they finish the sentence. Something rings hollow and they’re gone — the words weren’t wrong. Nobody meant them.

My only metric for any piece of communication is two questions. Do I believe it? Do I believe them?

I grew up around politics — where a line either survives contact with every kind of listener or it doesn’t run. Three novels. A screenplay. Thirteen years across DTC, SaaS, fintech, and AI. A childhood spent watching language move people toward decisions that shaped elections.

The portfolio is the answer to both questions.

01  ·  Original Concepts

Four campaigns built around the part of the audience everyone else forgot about.

Concept 01

The Wave.

For resilience-first consumer brands — health, wellness, financial recovery — that talk about strength but only ever show the triumphant after, never the difficult middle.

The Problem

Strength campaigns are built around the after. The person who made it. The breakthrough moment. The person still in it — not broken, not done, just still going — isn’t in the frame.

The Campaign
You’ve been knocked down enough times
to stop counting.
Point at the next one.
Executions
Billboard

The wave is coming anyway.  [Brand]

Paid Social — Short

You stopped asking if you could handle it. You already know you can.  [Brand]

Paid Social — Medium

There’s a specific kind of person who has been through enough that fear stopped being the deciding factor. They don’t feel brave. They just stopped waiting to.  [Brand]

Video VO — 60s

You’ve been knocked down by enough of them to know what they feel like coming. The fear doesn’t go away. You just stop letting it decide. Point at the next one. [Brand]. For the ones still in it.

Concept 04

The Campaign

04  ·  Diagnostic Work

The diagnosis comes
before the copy.

Every one of these started with a different brief. Every one had the same problem underneath it.

01 — Feature-heavy product messaging

The product is the subject. The user is an afterthought.

Before
Our platform provides innovative solutions to streamline your workflow and enhance productivity outcomes.
After
You already know what’s slowing you down. We help you fix it — without adding three more tools to your stack.
The original describes the product. The rewrite describes the person using it.

02 — Luxury brand over-explaining

The copy is written for someone who needs convincing. The customer it's aimed at doesn't.

Before
Our products are crafted using the highest quality ingredients, carefully sourced from around the world to deliver results you can see and feel.
After
If you know, you know.
The original justifies the price. The rewrite assumes the customer already understands value.

03 — Transactional lifecycle messaging

A system confirming an action. Nothing more. An opportunity to say something real, missed.

Before
Your account has been successfully updated.
After
You’re all set. We’ll let you know if anything needs your attention.
The original confirms a transaction. The rewrite removes a worry.

04 — Re-engagement email leading with the task

The task is framed as incomplete. The belief that created the hesitation goes unaddressed.

Before
Complete your setup to start using [product].
After
Subject: You’re closer than you think. — You’re already set up. People start seeing results within minutes of logging back in. The only thing left is to take a look.
The original names what’s missing. The rewrite frames it as already handled.

05 — Health and wellness category reframe

The solution is named before the problem is correctly identified. Ten other brands are making the same claim.

Before
Boost your energy naturally with our scientifically formulated supplement.
After
Your energy is fine. Your processing is overloaded.
The original names the solution. The rewrite names a different problem.
05  ·  Brand Voice System

Building
what scales.

Don’t waste people’s time. They can’t get it back. Don’t waste your own — that’ll hurt worse.

Find the truest sentence. Everything else is noise.

Connection will always outperform confidence. Nobody wants to feel sold to. They want to be anticipated — or they’re the one seeking, and they want to find something or be found by it. The copy that knows which one it’s talking to is the copy that works.

Voice in Practice — One Brand, Every Context
Campaign headlineYou’ve been doing this the hard way.
Product pageThe [product] that does what you’ve been trying to get everything else to do.
Onboarding — screen oneLet’s get you set up. This part is fast.
ConfirmationYou’re all set. We’ll take it from here.
Error stateSomething went wrong. Here’s how to fix it.
Empty stateNothing here yet. Everything changes the moment you [action].
Re-engagement emailYou’re closer than you think.
06  ·  Applied Work — Real Brands, Real Stakes

The copy wasn’t
in the job description.
I did it anyway.

Straps. Carecentra. Three hospitality venues. Each one run by people who are masters at what they do — the artistry, the science, the algorithm. My job is to help them explain themselves. To turn what feels like expertise too deep to communicate into a conversation someone actually wants to have.

Rosemary & Beauty Queen

Nashville · On-site General Manager · rosemaryandbeautyqueen.com
The Role

On-site General Manager — operations, inventory, staff, and special events. Menu development and cocktail concepts were part of running the place. A GM who understands the brand is the only one who can write a menu the room will actually sell.

The Copy Work

Original cocktail concepts and menu development. REMAKE/REMODEL. THREE & NINE. AVALON. Each name is a brief that fits in three words: what it tastes like, what it feels like, and what it says about where you are.

Copy feedback arrives weeks later, filtered through a client, diluted by distance. In hospitality it arrives at 5:30, at 8pm, at closing. The menu is the campaign. The register tells you whether it worked. And the menu is only part of it. The lighting is copy. The music is copy. The energy you want the room to have at 10pm on a Saturday is a brief, and every element in the space is either serving it or working against it. Turn the lights up and put on the wrong music and the room empties itself. The approach was always the same: start with the vision of what the room should be and work backward. The path presents itself when you know where you’re going.

Every word has to be sellable by a server at 9pm on a Saturday.

IGC Hospitality

NYC & Orlando · Floor Manager, high-volume operations · igchospitality.com
The Role

Floor manager, 30+ staff per night across multiple concepts — Park Avenue Tavern, The Refinery Rooftop, The Wilson, Take Care, Winnie’s Jazz Bar. Managing at that scale means reading the room constantly and understanding what each concept owes its customer.

The Copy Work

Original cocktail concepts and menu development across five distinct venues. A rooftop bar in Midtown and a jazz bar downtown don’t share a customer or a voice. Writing for both in the same week, making neither sound like the other.

Flamingo Cocktail Club

Nashville · General Manager — full operational purview

I rewrote the menus, developed the cocktail concepts, wrote the social copy, and made the brand say what the room actually was. Whether it landed showed up the same night.

Straps Leather

DTC leather brand · Founded & launched · strapsleather.com

Full brand launch from zero. Named the company, defined the product line, built the site. Every word with my own money behind it and my own name on the door.

Carecentra / Prometheus Holdings

New York · Freelance Copywriter · 2016 · Developed in collaboration with Stanford University, MIT, and Princeton’s Machine Learning Lab
The Brief

Carecentra used predictive algorithms to identify patients at risk of future health events — before symptoms appeared — and move them toward preventative care journeys. My job was to write the copy that made someone act on that prediction.

The Problem

A patient being asked to act on a prediction about their own body — made by a system they couldn’t see, about a future they hadn’t yet felt. Do it without frightening them. Do it without sounding like a scam. Earn credibility for an algorithm before a single symptom had appeared.

The Copy Work

A patient being asked to act on an algorithmic prediction about their own body — before they feel anything wrong — has no existing pain to name, no urgency to reach. The copy has to create the reason from a prediction alone. Every line written against two questions: do they believe the claim, and do they believe the people making it?

Also wrote financial communications for Prometheus Capital Partners, the company’s Panama-based subsidiary — translating complex lending products and financial analytics into clear narratives for banking clients and government partners across Central America.

I solve problems that aren’t my own. Solutions appear. The path from where something is to where it should be presents itself — sometimes before anyone’s asked me to look.

Hospitality taught me to anticipate. Politics reinforced it. You learn what people will do before they do it through study, repetition, failure, and always paying more attention than you let on.

Thirteen years of copy work is the same discipline applied to language.
